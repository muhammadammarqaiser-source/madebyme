Industry Trends & Local Context

Dark Fiber — Why it matters

Dark fiber leasing gives organizations control over their physical medium, stronger security, and dedicated bandwidth. It's widely adopted by enterprises and government departments for private networks and inter-datacenter links.

FTTH & GPON evolution

GPON (ITU-T G.984) remains the standard for many deployments; newer XG-PON (10Gbps) is rolling out for higher capacity. GPON split ratios and ONT quality determine customer experience.

Bandwidth & CDN

Combining raw bandwidth with CDN caching reduces backbone costs and improves response time for media-heavy content and software distribution — a must for modern ISPs.

Backbone upgrades & national fiber programs expanding deep fiberization
Regulatory guidance encourages secure dark-fiber use for sensitive government data
Enterprise GPON & XG-PON adoption for multi-gigabit services

Quick Specs & Best Practices

  • Fiber types: Single-mode (G.652) preferred for long-haul & FTTH.
  • GPON speeds: Down ~2.488 Gbps / Up ~1.244 Gbps (per GPON); XG-PON offers 10 Gbps classes.
  • Splitters: Passive split ratios commonly 1:16 or 1:32 for GPON deployments.
  • OSP: Use ducts, micro-trenching and aerial where appropriate; ensure splice points and testing rigs are documented.
